Green Tea

Made from unoxidized leaves and known for its fresh flavor and numerous health benefits due to its high antioxidant content.

Black Tea

Fully oxidized leaves, resulting in a robust flavor and darker color. Common varieties include Assam, Darjeeling, and Earl Grey.

Oolong Tea

Partially oxidized leaves, offering a flavor profile somewhere between green and black tea. It's often associated with floral or fruity notes.

White Tea

Made from young leaves and buds that are minimally processed, resulting in a delicate flavor and light color. Silver Needle and White Peony are popular varieties.

Herbal Tea

Not technically a tea, as it doesn't come from the Camellia sinensis plant, herbal teas are infusions made from various herbs, flowers, spices, and fruits. Examples include chamomile, peppermint, and rooibos.

Yellow Tea

A rare and delicate type of tea with a slightly sweet flavor profile, produced through a unique processing method involving a slight oxidation and a short period of "yellowing" after drying.

Pu-erh Tea

A fermented tea originating from China, known for its earthy flavor and potential health benefits. It can be aged like wine, with its flavor improving over time.
